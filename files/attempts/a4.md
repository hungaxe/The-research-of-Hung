# The Proposed Vision


"I am proposing a structurally-aware mathematical platform designed to natively ingest and process N operations within the domain of general convolutions—not by computing them sequentially to scalar outputs, but by constructing a formal algebraic system where N  convolution expressions exist as irreducible structural entities rather than as aggregates awaiting decomposition.

In traditional environments, each of the N  convolutions must be reduced through iteration: input → scalar output → comparison → synthesis. This process necessarily strips the structural relationships that distinguish the convolutions from one another. My framework reverses this: rather than decomposing first and then attempting to recover structure, it preserves structure as the primary mathematical object by embedding these N  convolutions into a rigorously defined space where their algebraic relationships—symmetries, hierarchical dependencies, and topological coherence—are encoded directly into the operations themselves.

The key difference is semantic preservation under closure: whereas traditional computation requires O(N)  materializations of scalar results before any equivalence or structural comparison is possible, this framework allows the N  convolutions to participate in higher-order operations (composition, comparison, equivalence-checking) while maintaining their convolutional form. This is not merely a representation trick; it is a genuine change in what constitutes an irreducible computational unit.

By strictly bounding this model to general convolutions, we avoid undecidability and overgeneralization. But within this bounded scope, we achieve something revolutionary: a proof that for this problem class, the requirement to decompose to scalars and recompose is not a fundamental constraint of mathematics, but a consequence of our chosen representation. A structurally-coherent alternative exists, where the convolutions themselves become the basis of computation rather than obstacles to it." - Hung Dinh Phu Dang -

"We propose investigating a mathematical environment where N convolution expressions $\sum_i a_i \cdot b_i, \sum_j c_j \cdot d_j, ..., \sum_k z_k \cdot w_k$ are treated as primitive symbolic objects rather than abbreviations for their scalar evaluations. In this environment, algebraic operations (composition, tensor formation, functional application) are defined directly on the expression structure, preserving the relationships and symmetries that become opaque once convolutions are reduced to scalars. The conjecture is that for specific problem classes—particularly those involving multiple parameterized convolutions with shared structure (e.g., neural network layers, signal processing pipelines, constraint systems)—this symbolic-first representation admits both simpler notation and computational shortcuts (e.g., early termination, gradient-free optimization, structural equivalence testing) that are invisible in the scalar regime. This does not claim universal speedup or new computational fundamentals; rather, it claims that convolution-structured problems may have a natural representation layer where their inherent redundancy becomes tractable in ways blocked by premature scalar reduction." - Hung Dinh Phu Dang -

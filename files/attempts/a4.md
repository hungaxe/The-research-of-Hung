# The Proposed Vision

"I am proposing a structurally-aware mathematical platform designed to natively ingest and process $N$ operations within the domain of general convolutions. In traditional algebraic environments, executing $N$ convolutions necessitates iteratively decomposing the inputs into $N$ scalar outputs—a computational artifact that fundamentally strips away the original structural semantics. To address this, my framework introduces a predefined topological space where these $N$ convolutional expressions are embedded directly as fundamental entities. Rather than forcing immediate scalar reduction, the platform evaluates them within this novel environment, allowing the inputs to retain an algebraic significance analogous to traditional mathematics while preserving their overarching structural relationships. By strictly bounding this model to general convolutions, we maintain mathematical rigor and deliberately avoid the undecidability of universal computational claims. This targeted preservation of structural integrity provides a highly feasible yet fundamentally revolutionary paradigm, unlocking entirely new, structurally-coherent applications in domains heavily reliant on convolutional processing." - Hung Dinh Phu Dang -


I am proposing a structurally-aware mathematical platform designed to natively ingest and process N operations within the domain of general convolutions—not by computing them sequentially to scalar outputs, but by constructing a formal algebraic system where N  convolution expressions exist as irreducible structural entities rather than as aggregates awaiting decomposition.
In traditional environments, each of the N  convolutions must be reduced through iteration: input → scalar output → comparison → synthesis. This process necessarily strips the structural relationships that distinguish the convolutions from one another. My framework reverses this: rather than decomposing first and then attempting to recover structure, it preserves structure as the primary mathematical object by embedding these N  convolutions into a rigorously defined space where their algebraic relationships—symmetries, hierarchical dependencies, and topological coherence—are encoded directly into the operations themselves.
The key difference is semantic preservation under closure: whereas traditional computation requires O(N)  materializations of scalar results before any equivalence or structural comparison is possible, this framework allows the N  convolutions to participate in higher-order operations (composition, comparison, equivalence-checking) while maintaining their convolutional form. This is not merely a representation trick; it is a genuine change in what constitutes an irreducible computational unit.
By strictly bounding this model to general convolutions, we avoid undecidability and overgeneralization. But within this bounded scope, we achieve something revolutionary: a proof that for this problem class, the requirement to decompose to scalars and recompose is not a fundamental constraint of mathematics, but a consequence of our chosen representation. A structurally-coherent alternative exists, where the convolutions themselves become the basis of computation rather than obstacles to it.
